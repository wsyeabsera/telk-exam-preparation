# Capitalization Rules (Groß- und Kleinschreibung)

In German, **nouns** and **Sie** (formal "you") are always capitalized. This is a recurring B1 error.

---

## Always Capitalized

1. **All nouns:** der **M**ann, die **F**rau, das **K**ind, **D**eutsch (the language), im **S**ommer, am **M**ontag.
2. **Sie, Ihr, Ihnen** (formal "you" and its forms): **K**önnen **S**ie mir helfen? **I**hre **A**ntwort. **I**hnen.
3. **Nominalized verbs/adjectives:** das **L**esen, das **G**ute, beim **S**chwimmen.

**Deutsche Erklärung:** Nomen und die Anrede **Sie/Ihr/Ihnen** schreibt man groß. Alle Nomen: **M**ann, **S**ommer, **D**eutsch.

---

## Not Capitalized (Unless Noun or Sie)

- **sie** (she / they), **ihr** (her / their / you pl.), **ihnen** (them) when not formal — lowercase.
- **er, sie, es, wir, ihr** — lowercase.
- Adjectives when they are adjectives: *ein **g**uter Tag* (gut is adjective, Tag is noun and capitalized only as noun).

**Common mistake:** *im **s**ommer* → **im Sommer**. *Ich danke **I**hnen* (formal) vs. *Ich danke **i**hr* (her).

---

## Quick Check

- Every noun: first letter capital.
- Formal "you": **Sie, Ihr, Ihnen** always capital.
- Pronoun "she/they": **sie** lowercase (unless start of sentence).
